# Budgets and fees

The budget is not administrative overhead attached to the real application. **It is the application, restated in numbers** — and for CALQ it is a pass/fail gate ("Satisfactory" minimum on the administrative evaluation) that can eliminate an artistically strong project outright.

A budget's job: **demonstrate feasibility and cohesion.** A jury reads a budget to find out whether the artist actually knows what the project costs.

---

## Rules

1. **It must add up.** Check the arithmetic. Then check it again. An error here is disproportionately fatal because it is the one thing on the form that is objectively, checkably wrong.
2. **Expenses and revenues must balance.** Total revenue = total expenses, netting to zero (or a modest surplus where the funder allows it).
3. **Avoid a deficit budget.** Projected expenses exceeding projected revenues tells the jury the project cannot happen.
4. **Itemize.** Quantity × unit cost × units = line total, grouped into subtotalled categories. `Materials — $4,000` is a red flag. `Linen canvas, 12 m × $38/m = $456` is evidence.
5. **Research the real numbers.** Call suppliers, shippers, framers, crating companies. Do not estimate from vibes; juries in your discipline know what things cost and will notice.
6. **Never budget the same expense to two funders.** CALQ states plainly it will not support the same expense twice. If a presenter covers your fee or a residency covers lodging, that is *their* line, not yours.
7. **Contingency: 5–10%** is normal and reads as competence — where the funder permits it.
8. **Most funders do not fund capital/infrastructure** — equipment purchases and assets. Rent, don't buy. (Some programs have explicit "equip myself" streams — CAM has one. Check.)
9. **Taxes:** if you don't have a GST/QST number, taxes are typically a real cost to you and belong in the budget. If you're registered and claim input tax credits, they don't. Flag where you've included them.
10. **Some funders cap their share.** CALQ *Circulation Outside Quebec* funds a maximum of **75% of total project costs** — meaning the other 25% must visibly come from somewhere. Check the cap for every component.
11. **In-kind donations go in both columns.** Donated studio space, loaned equipment, a gallery's free install crew: enter the fair value as both an expense and a revenue so it cancels out. This shows the project's true *monetary* value while preserving its true *cash* value — and it demonstrates partner buy-in.
12. **The artist's own contribution is legitimate and often desirable.** It signals investment in the work. It is not a confession of poverty.
13. **You may request the maximum** — but only if the scope genuinely merits it. A maxed-out request attached to a thin project is transparent.

## Expense categories (visual arts)

- **Artist fee / subsistence** — your labour and living costs during the project. Not optional. See below.
- **Assistant / technician / collaborator fees** — at fair rates, named where possible
- **Materials and supplies** — itemized, sourced
- **Studio rent / production space**
- **Equipment rental** (not purchase)
- **Fabrication and external services** — foundry, printing, framing, editioning
- **Documentation** — professional photography/video of the work; often forgotten, always needed for the *next* application
- **Transport / crating / shipping / customs / insurance** — for anything leaving the studio
- **Travel** — flights, ground transport, visas, vaccinations, travel insurance
- **Accommodation and per diem** — CALQ caps per diem + lodging at **$200/day per participant** on Travel and Circulation grants
- **Promotion / publicity** — where not covered by the host
- **Administrative costs** — CALQ Circulation allows up to **10% of total project budget**
- **Translation** — of texts, statements, didactic material (a real cost for Quebec artists working in Asia or ROC)
- **Contingency** — 5–10%

## Revenue categories

- Grant requested (this application)
- Other grants: **confirmed** vs **pending** vs **to be requested** — label the status honestly; funders check
- Artist's own contribution
- Presenter/venue fees, exhibition fees, sales
- Sponsorships, in-kind (matched in expenses)

## CARFAC-RAAV: the fee anchor for visual artists

The **CARFAC-RAAV Minimum Recommended Fee Schedule** is the national standard for what visual and media artists are paid for copyright and professional services. Using it does three things: pays the artist properly, makes the budget instantly credible to a visual-arts jury, and satisfies the "fair and equitable compensation" test CALQ applies administratively.

**2026 exhibition royalties** — solo exhibition, up to 3 months (CAD, pre-tax), by presenting institution's operating-budget category:

| Category (institution's operating budget) | Solo | Retrospective (+15%) | Project (−15%) | Single work (20%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **International II** | $18,925 | $21,763 | $16,087 | $3,786 |
| **International I** | $12,627 | $14,520 | $10,733 | $2,525 |
| **Category III** (> $1M) | $4,208 | $4,838 | $3,577 | $864 |
| **Category II** ($500,001–$1M) | $3,432 | $3,948 | $2,918 | $686 |
| **Category I** (< $500,000) | $2,574 | $2,960 | $2,189 | $514 |

**Group exhibition, up to 3 months, per artist** (selected):

| Category | 2 artists | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6–10 | 11–15 | 16–29 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **International II** | $13,248 | $9,463 | $5,677 | $4,731 | $3,785 | $3,217 | $2,839 |
| **International I** | $8,839 | $6,313 | $3,788 | $3,157 | $2,525 | $2,147 | $1,894 |
| **Category III** | $2,945 | $2,104 | $1,263 | $1,052 | $841 | $714 | $631 |
| **Category II** | $2,402 | $1,716 | $1,030 | $858 | $686 | $583 | $515 |
| **Category I** | $1,802 | $1,288 | $773 | $644 | $515 | $438 | $386 |

Longer runs scale up: a five-month solo at Category III is **$6,312** ($4,208 base + $1,052 × 2 additional months). Flat rates apply to 4–6, 7–9, and 10–12 month runs; over 12 months is negotiated at no less than the 12-month rate.

The schedule also covers **artist professional services** (talks, workshops, jury duty, curatorial work) and **public art** in separate sections. Rates are updated annually — **verify at carfac-raav.ca before every budget.** All figures exclude applicable taxes.

Note the category is set by the *institution's* operating budget (total revenues averaged over three years), not the exhibition's. An artist-run centre is usually Category I; a major museum, Category III or International.

## Common budget failures

| Failure | Fix |
|---|---|
| No artist fee | Pay yourself. CARFAC-RAAV for exhibition; a defensible day/hour rate for production time. |
| Round numbers throughout | Source real quotes. |
| Doesn't balance / doesn't add up | Recalculate. Every time. |
| Deficit | Reduce scope or find the missing revenue — don't submit it. |
| Same expense claimed twice across funders | Split cleanly, disclose all sources. |
| Equipment purchase in a program that funds no capital | Rent it, or find the right program. |
| No documentation line | Add it. You will need images for the next grant, the next show, the archive. |
| No contingency on an international project | Currency, customs, and shipping will surprise you. |
| Per diem over the cap | CALQ: $200/day all-in for lodging + meals. |
| Ignoring the funder's maximum share | If the cap is 75%, show the other 25%. |

## After the grant

Keep every receipt and invoice — for the funder's final report and for Revenu Québec / CRA. **Grant money is taxable income**; associated expenses may be deductible. Track actuals against the budget as you go. Surplus is generally yours *if* you fulfilled the terms of the application — but if the money wasn't used as proposed, it may have to be returned. Any material change to the project should be reported to the program officer, who may need to approve it.

**File final reports on time.** Overdue reports make you ineligible for new applications at both CCA and CALQ. This is the single most avoidable way artists lock themselves out of funding.
