## Canada Council for the Arts (CCA) — federal

### The 2025 restructure (critical — most advice online is out of date)

The Council **rebuilt its granting architecture in 2025**. Program names in older blog posts, workshop decks, and even some artist-run-centre resources are now wrong. Current top-level programs:

1. **Explore and Create**
2. **Engage and Sustain**
3. **Creating, Knowing and Sharing** — First Nations, Inuit and Métis peoples
4. **Supporting Artistic Practice**
5. **Arts Across Canada and Abroad** ← merges the former *Arts Abroad* and *Arts Across Canada*

**Explore and Create** now contains **Artistic Creation** — which absorbed the former **Research and Creation** *and* **Concept to Realization** components. If an artist says "I'm applying to Research and Creation," gently correct them: that component no longer exists as such. Deadline for Artistic Creation is **any time before the project start date** (rolling).

**Arts Across Canada and Abroad** funding opportunities include: Micro-grants; Representation and Promotion; Translation; International Translation; Circulation and Touring; **International Residencies**; International Co-productions; Foreign Artist Tours; Public Outreach; Arts Festivals and Presenters. Several are fixed-deadline (Circulation and Touring and International Residencies had multiple rounds in 2026; verify current dates).

### How CCA decides

- **Peer assessment.** Committees of working artists/professionals, selected to reflect Canada's regional, cultural, linguistic, disciplinary, age, gender and accessibility diversity. **Exception:** some components (e.g. *Representation and Promotion*) are **internally assessed by Council staff**, not by a peer committee. The guidelines say which. It changes how you write.
- **Scoring.** Each component uses **2 to 4 criteria totalling 100**, each with a **minimum threshold**. Weightings and thresholds **vary by component** — there is no house split. Verified figures for four components are in **`cca-components.md`**; for anything else, pull the guidelines.
- **RECAP** — *Respectful and Ethical Cultural and Artistic Practices* — is a **pass/fail gate on every component**, on top of the scored criteria.
- **Preliminary assessment** narrows the field for project grants before discussion and ranking. Core/operating grants skip this step.
- **Comparative ranking.** Applications are ranked in order of merit against each other, not against an absolute standard.
- **Context briefs** — written documents given to assessors to help them understand emerging, minoritized, Indigenous, culturally diverse, and Deaf/disability practices. Relevant if a practice sits outside a jury's default frame.
- **Dedicated funds** are reserved for priority groups: Deaf and disability communities; Black and racialized artists; First Nations, Inuit and Métis peoples; official-language minority communities. The peer ranking order is respected when awarding these.
- **Approval authority** by amount: manager ≤$30k; director ≤$100k; Director General $100,001–$500,000; Board >$500,000.
- **Decisions are final.** No appeal unless a procedural error occurred. Applicants receive a **score breakdown and written feedback** — always request it.

### Governing principles (state them back in the artist's own terms, never parrot them)

Arm's-length from government · stewardship of public funds · peer assessment · respectful workplaces (free from discrimination, harassment, sexual misconduct) · cultural self-determination of Indigenous peoples · equity and diversity · funding in all parts of Canada · official languages · comparative assessment · outcome-based approach.

### Practical CCA notes
- Requires an approved **applicant profile** in the portal *before* applying — this takes time and is a common cause of missed deadlines. Verify the artist has one at intake.
- **Application Assistance** funds are available to cover the cost of services needed to complete an application (e.g. for Deaf, disabled, or first-time applicants). These are **separate from grant funds** — do not fold them into the project budget.
- English-speaking artists in Quebec are an **official-language minority community**. Complete the voluntary self-identification form; specific envelopes exist.

## CALQ — Conseil des arts et des lettres du Québec (provincial)

### Eligibility (individual artists)
- Canadian citizen or permanent resident, **normally resident in Quebec for the past 12 months**.
- **Students graduating from higher-education arts institutions are no longer eligible** (change effective September 2023).
- CALQ **abolished experience-based categories** ("emerging," "established"). Status is assessed on total years of artistic experience and the jury's judgment — not credentials. Do not write "as an emerging artist…"; it maps to nothing.
- **Volume caps:** up to **5 applications and 5 grants per fiscal year** (April 1 – March 31), but **no more than 3 projects under evaluation, funded, and/or in progress at once**.

### The nine components of the Programme de bourses aux artistes
1. **Exploration and Research** — supports the risk inherent in research; experimentation prior to a new work, exhibition or publication
2. **Creation** — creation of works and projects advancing the practice
3. **Public Presentation** — presenting works/shows/exhibitions before an audience
4. **Travel** (*Bourse de déplacement*)
5. **Circulation of Works Within Quebec**
6. **Circulation of Works Outside Quebec**
7. **Professional Development**
8. **Publication and Critical Documentation**
9. **Strategic Opportunities and Career Planning** — atypical, career-decisive projects

Plus **Vivacité** (practices representative of cultural diversity) and the **Montréal studio-apartment** residency (reserved for Quebec artists living *outside* Montreal).

### How CALQ scores — the two-track model (important, and different from CCA)

CALQ separates **artistic evaluation** (scored) from **administrative evaluation** (rated pass/fail). Both must clear.

**Track 1 — Artistic evaluation = 100% of the score.** Always two criteria, but **the split changes by component.** Verified July 2026, profile *Artiste ou écrivain*, discipline *Arts visuels*:

| Component | Quality of artistic work | Interest & relevance of project |
|---|---|---|
| **Creation** | 30% | **70%** |
| **Exploration and Research** | 30% | **70%** |
| **Public Presentation** | 30% | **70%** |
| **Strategic Opportunities and Career Planning** | 30% | **70%** |
| **Travel** | 40% | 60% |
| **Circulation Within Quebec** | 40% | 60% |
| **Circulation Outside Quebec** | 40% | 60% |
| **Professional Development** | 40% | 60% |
| **Publication and Critical Documentation** | 40% | 60% |

**In every single component, the project outweighs the portfolio.** Most artists write these backwards — they spend the word count proving they are a good artist (worth 30–40%) and treat the project's value as self-evident (worth 60–70%).

What "interest and relevance" actually contains varies by component, and this is where applications are won:
- *Creation:* artistic value of the project · composition of the team · **impacts and spin-offs** · **effect on the evolution of the applicant's work**
- *Exploration and Research:* interest for the advancement of artistic practices · **novelty of the project** · **rigour and articulation of the research process** · effect on the evolution of the work
- *Travel:* **interest and impact on career development and the visibility of the work** · quality of the host organization's commitment
- *Circulation (in/out of Quebec):* quality and artistic interest of the activity presented · **relevance of partners and host structures** · **quality of the host organization's commitment**
- *Professional Development:* quality of the training program · **choice of host structure, master, or mentor** · effect on the evolution of the work/career
- *Publication:* interest of the author's approach · relevance and involvement of partners · **quality of promotion and distribution strategy**

**Track 2 — Administrative evaluation.** Two criteria, both of which must be rated at least **"Satisfactory"**:
1. **Remuneration of artists and collaborators** — fairness of the applicant's own pay, and of the team's
2. **Project management** — realism of the work plan, realism of the budget (and, for commissions, the significance of partner contributions)

Rating scales differ: some components use a binary *Satisfaisant / Non satisfaisant*; others (Travel, Circulation, Publication, Strategic Opportunities) use a five-point scale — *Excellent – Très bon – Satisfaisant – Comportant des réserves – Faible* — with **"Satisfaisant" as the floor**.

The practical consequence: **a beautiful project with a sloppy budget or an underpaid artist is not scored down — it is knocked out.** Track 2 is a gate, not a tiebreaker.

**Each CALQ program page links the actual jury scoring grid ("grille d'évaluation," a small PDF/DOC).** Fetch it — it is the literal instrument the jury fills in.

### Reading CALQ criteria yourself

Criteria are rendered **only after selecting Profil + Discipline** on the program page (a TYPO3 POST form — URL query parameters are silently ignored, so you cannot link directly to them). Some pages render criteria without selection; others require it. IDs: *Artiste ou écrivain* = `21`, *Commissaire* = `22`; *Arts visuels* = `38`, *Arts numériques* = `37`, *Arts multidisciplinaires* = `36`, *Cinéma et vidéo* = `40`, *Métiers d'art* = `44`, *Recherche architecturale* = `47`.

The French pages (`/aides/<slug>/`) carry the criteria; the English pages often do not. Slugs: `creation`, `recherche-exploration`, `presentation-publique`, `deplacement`, `circulation-au-quebec`, `circulation-hors-quebec`, `au-perfectionnement`, `publication-documentation-critique`, `opportunites-strategiques-plan-carriere`.

### CALQ logistics
- **Rolling deadlines** ("en tout temps") on most artist grants; must submit **at least 4 weeks before activities begin**.
- **Response ~8 weeks** after submission (the FAQ also states decisions within four months of application — plan for the longer figure).
- **CALQ will not fund the same expense twice.** If a presenter is paying the artist fee, do not also claim it.
- Reports due within **3 months** of project end (circulation).
- Score breakdowns per criterion are available on request after a decision.

## Conseil des arts de Montréal (CAM) — municipal

~59 programs, filterable by profile (artists / collectives / cultural workers / organizations), discipline, and type of aid ("carry out a project," "develop my practice," "equip myself"). Montreal residency/practice is the gating condition. **CAM is the most under-used of the three levels** — artists routinely apply federally and provincially and skip the municipal layer entirely. Check `artsmontreal.org/en/grants/programs` at intake.

## The stacking strategy

The three levels are **designed to be combined**, and diversity of funding sources is itself part of CALQ's administrative assessment. A realistic visual-arts project might run: CALQ *Creation* for studio time and materials → CAM for production/presentation in Montreal → CCA *Arts Across Canada and Abroad* for the international leg.

Rules that constrain stacking:
- **Never budget the same expense to two funders.** Disclose all other applications, confirmed or pending — funders talk to each other, and the budget must show the full financial picture regardless.
- **CCA:** historically limited artists to one grant to professional artists per fiscal year in some streams, and blocks new applications while final reports are overdue. **Verify the current rule in the component guidelines — do not assert it.**
- **CALQ:** 3 in-flight / 5 per year caps above.
- **Overdue final reports make you ineligible.** Everywhere. Check this first at intake.

## Other funders to check for a Quebec-based artist

- **SODEC** — cultural *enterprises*, not individual artists (people confuse this constantly)
- **Ministère des Relations internationales et de la Francophonie (MRIF)** + the **Délégations générales du Québec** abroad (incl. Tokyo) — co-fund the CALQ international residencies; also relevant for mobility support
- **Canadian Heritage** — official-language minority community projects
- **Private:** Hnatyshyn Foundation, Chalmers, Bronfman, Sobey Art Award (nomination-based)
- **Artist-run centres and regional partnership agreements** (CALQ runs territorial partnership programs with most Quebec regions — different deadlines, much less competition)
- **ELAN** (English Language Arts Network) and **YES Montreal** — free/low-cost grant-writing workshops, coaching, and accounting clinics for Quebec artists; RAAV for Quebec visual artists
