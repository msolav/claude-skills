# Question-by-question answering guide

The actual fields, in the order the forms present them, with what each one is really testing. Verified from CCA application-form previews and CALQ application forms.

---

## Structural facts that change how you write

**1. The jury does not see who you are — on paper.** CALQ's personal-information form states: *"This document is confidential and will be withdrawn during evaluation of the application."* CCA's identification section: *"The information that you provide here will not be submitted to the peer assessment committee."*

Your name, address, and contact details are stripped. Your **CV and work samples are not.** So identity is partially known but *status is not asserted for you* — nothing in front of the jury says "this is an important artist." The application has to carry itself.

**2. Unsolicited material is discarded.** CALQ: *"Unsolicited documents will not be submitted to the evaluation committee."* CCA: missing *required* material makes you **ineligible**. So: send exactly what is asked, and everything that is asked. Extra pages are not read; missing pages are fatal.

**3. Every section is labelled with the criteria it feeds.** CCA prints this on the form ("Assessment criteria for this section: Artistic criteria · Impact criteria"). **That is the scoring key.** Answer each question in the register of the criterion it serves — a Feasibility question wants evidence and dates, not artistic vision.

**4. You must declare parallel applications.** CALQ: *"I am required to declare any grant applications submitted to another organization for the same expenses."* Do not try to be clever about this.

## Canada Council — Artistic Creation (1006) and its siblings

### "Describe your proposed activities." — 7,000 characters *(Artistic + Impact criteria)*

This is the whole project description: ~1,100 words carrying **80% of the score** (Artistic 50 + Impact 30). The Council prints the questions it wants answered. Treat them as a checklist and make sure each one is visibly addressed:

| Their prompt | Criterion it feeds | What a strong answer does |
|---|---|---|
| What exactly are you planning to do, and how? | Artistic | Medium, scale, quantity, technique, materials. Concrete nouns. |
| What is the artistic and/or cultural rationale? | **Artistic `/20`** | The *why*. This is a full fifth of the Artistic score and is where most applications go vague. |
| What are some of your inspirations? | Artistic | Specific — artists, texts, events, sites. Not "nature and memory." |
| Does your work include taking artistic risks? If so, how? | Artistic | **Say yes and name the risk.** The Council is inviting you to declare uncertainty; refusing the invitation reads as timid. |
| Why is your project important **at this time**? | Artistic `/10` (relevance and timeliness) | Timeliness is scored explicitly. Answer it explicitly. |
| What are the potential artistic outcomes? | Artistic | What will exist that doesn't now. |
| How will this contribute to your artistic development? | **Impact `/15`** | The evolution-of-practice argument. |
| Any accessibility measures for artists or audiences? | Impact | If yes, they are also **fundable budget lines**. |
| Who are your intended audiences or communities? | Impact | Can include "your community of practice" — a studio-based artist is not disqualified here. |
| How will you engage them? | Impact | Mechanism, not aspiration. |
| How will your project benefit artists, participants, audiences, communities? | **Impact `/15`** | Directly worded from the rubric. Mirror the language. |
| Are you addressing broader social issues (environment, accessibility, social justice)? | Impact | **Only if true.** Manufactured social relevance is the most transparent move in grant writing. |

**Budget your 7,000 characters against the weights.** Artistic is 50 and Impact is 30, so roughly 60/40 of the space. Do not spend 2,000 characters on inspirations and 300 on rationale.

### "What is your capacity and experience to carry out the activities?" — 3,500 chars *(Impact + Feasibility)*
Prompts: *Have you carried out similar activities in the past? What activities have prepared you to undertake this work?*
This is not a CV in prose. It is an argument that **this specific project is within reach** — ideally by pointing at the closest thing you have already completed. If the project is a stretch, name the stretch and name what closes the gap (a collaborator, a technician, a host, a prior test).

### "Does your project involve collaborators or partners?" → then 1,700 chars
Prompts: *Do they have relevant experience? How will they help you succeed / reach your audiences? **Have they confirmed their participation?***
**The written confirmation from each partner uploads in the Support Material section, not here.** Forgetting that is an ineligibility, not a lost point.

### "What is your project timeline? Provide important milestones." — 3,500 chars *(Feasibility)*
Prompts: *Does your timeline give you enough time? Include key milestones and approximate dates.* Phases, dates, outputs. See `writing-craft.md`.

### RECAP — four yes/no questions, each with a 1,700-char follow-up
*Does your project involve: participation of First Nations, Inuit or Métis individuals or communities · their intellectual property or traditional/cultural knowledge · sensitive, triggering or traumatic topics · customary traditions or sacred ceremonies?*

If **yes** to any, you must demonstrate: authentic and respectful engagement efforts; that activities respect Indigenous protocols, principles and intellectual property; appropriate harm-reduction strategies. The form states plainly: *"Your application may be unsuccessful if you fail to demonstrate these points."*

If **no** to all — answer no, honestly, and move on. RECAP is specific to First Nations, Inuit and Métis content; it is not a general ethics questionnaire.

## CALQ — the general grant application

### Three documents, always
1. **Personal information form** *(withdrawn before evaluation)* — includes the declaration of other funding applications
2. **Project description form**
3. **Budget**

### Fields that trip people up

- **"Year artistic practice started."** This single number determines how the jury reads *"consistent with the candidate's level of experience"* in the remuneration rubric. It is also how CALQ handles experience now that "emerging/established" categories are abolished. Be accurate and be consistent across applications.
- **"Is the field indicated the one in which you are pursuing your career?"** A yes/no with teeth — a mismatch between your declared discipline and your CV invites an eligibility question.
- **Summary — 250 characters.** Not words. **Characters.** This is the sentence that gets repeated in the room. Write it last. See `writing-craft.md`.
- **Amount requested vs total cost.** On *Circulation Outside Québec* the form itself states the request "may not exceed 75% of the total cost." The form will expose an over-ask instantly.
- **"Does the project require the use of digital technologies (excluding office automation)?"** Answer honestly; it routes your file.

### Required documents — Circulation (representative of CALQ's document discipline)
- Personal information form, completed **and signed**
- Project description form
- **Applicant's CV — maximum 3 pages**
- List of participating artists and collaborators
- **CVs of participants and principal collaborators — max 3 pages each**
- **Contract or letter of commitment confirming at least one recognized presenting venue** ← without this, there is no application
- Estimate for shipping works or equipment, if applicable
- **Technical description of works and shipping method** (required for visual arts, digital arts, film/video, crafts)
- Press file — **maximum 5 pages**
- **Information on the structure of the host organization** ← feeds the scored "suitability of the host" sub-criterion

> *"The project, budget and the schedule must be present."* And: *"Unsolicited documents will not be submitted to the evaluation committee."*

## The ordering that works

1. **Interview** the artist; fill `assets/project-brief.md`.
2. **Write the long answer first** (CCA 7,000-char activities / CALQ project description). Everything else is derived from it.
3. **Timeline**, then **budget** — each one stress-tests the last.
4. **Partner/capacity answers**, using the real confirmations you now hold.
5. **Support material**, curated to the 10-minute rule.
6. **Summary last** (CALQ: 250 characters).
7. **Demonstration pass:** for every scored sub-criterion, point at the sentence that demonstrates it. See `calq-rubrics.md` — *"not demonstrated"* is the dominant failure mode.
8. **Jury simulation** against the real weights.
