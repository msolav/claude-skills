# Canada Council: component-by-component

Extracted from official CCA application-form previews (guideline PDFs from the applicant portal), **verified July 2026**. Component ID numbers are the Council's own (`1006`, `6004`, etc.) — useful for searching the portal.

**Re-verify every cycle.** These changed wholesale in the 2025 restructure and will change again.

---

## The universal architecture

Every component shares the same shape:

- **Three weighted criteria totalling 100**, each with a **minimum score** that must be met. Failing *any* minimum disqualifies, regardless of total.
- **The criterion names and weights differ per component.** There is no house split. Do not carry a weighting from one component to another.
- **Plus RECAP** — *Respectful and Ethical Cultural and Artistic Practices* — a **pass/fail gate on every component**: "Proper attention is paid to First Nations, Inuit or Métis cultural protocols, and cultural materials are treated in a respectful way — OR — there is no Indigenous content in the activities." An application with Indigenous content that mishandles protocol is **not funded**, whatever it scores.
- **The form tells you which criterion each question feeds.** Each section is labelled "Assessment criteria for this section: Artistic / Impact / Feasibility." **This is the scoring key, printed on the form.** Answer each question in the register of the criterion it serves.
- **Budget must balance to $0** where a balanced budget is required. Subtotals are **not auto-calculated** — do the arithmetic yourself.
- **Budget Notes fields** are the place to state how an amount was calculated and whether a revenue is confirmed or pending. Use them; they go to the committee.
- **Access Support** (disability-related supports) is a **separate request**, not a budget line.
- **Missing required support material = ineligible.** Not scored down. Ineligible.

## Assessment criteria by component

### 1006 — Artistic Creation *(Explore and Create)*
Peer assessment committee.

| Criterion | Weight | Min | Sub-criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Artistic** | **50%** | 35/50 | Artistic quality of your work + potential artistic outcomes `/20` · Artistic/cultural rationale `/20` · Relevance and timeliness within your practice or community `/10` |
| **Impact** | 30% | 15/30 | How the project advances your practice and career `/15` · Potential to benefit artists, participants, audiences, communities `/15` |
| **Feasibility** | 20% | 10/20 | Capacity and experience, including realistic timeline `/10` · Detailed budget showing **fair payment** and efficient use of resources `/10` |

- **Up to $75,000 per application**; max **$150,000/year**; **3 applications/year** (Jan 1–Dec 31).
- Eligible: creation, production, artists-and-community collaboration, public sharing (public sharing *must* accompany one of the others).
- **Ineligible:** projects mainly focused on public sharing/dissemination/touring **without an element of creation**; translation projects.
- Note the minimum on Artistic is **35/50 — a 70% threshold on the biggest criterion.** This is the hardest gate in the system. A merely competent portfolio fails it.

### 6605 — International Residencies *(Arts Across Canada and Abroad)*
Peer assessment committee.

| Criterion | Weight | Min | Sub-criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Relevance** | **50%** | 35/50 | Artistic quality of previous work `/15` · **Rationale for the project** `/15` · **Suitability and timeliness** `/20` |
| **Impact** | 30% | 15/30 | Potential to develop/enhance your international profile and Canadian arts practice `/10` · Build international connections and exchange knowledge `/10` · Generate future opportunities `/10` |
| **Feasibility** | 20% | 10/20 | **Suitability and involvement of the host organization** `/10` · Capacity and experience of you *and the host* `/5` · Reasonable budget, fair payment `/5` |

- **Up to $20,000**; **2 applications/year**.
- **Ineligible:** hosting a residency; activities taking place in Canada.
- **Start date must be after the application deadline.**
- Only expenses **not covered by the host**, and only those **incurred over the duration of the residency**, are eligible.
- **Read the weighting:** your previous work is worth only `/15` of 100. The *host*, the *rationale*, and the *timeliness* carry the application. Choosing the right residency and proving the host is genuinely engaged matters more than your portfolio.

### 6004 — Circulation and Touring *(Arts Across Canada and Abroad)*
Peer assessment committee. **Impact leads here — the only component where it does.**

| Criterion | Weight | Min | Sub-criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | **40%** | 25/40 | Enhance the public's appreciation of the arts `/15` · Develop/enhance your profile or practice `/15` · Generate future opportunities `/10` |
| **Artistic** | 30% | 15/30 | Artistic quality of the work `/20` · **Suitability of the work for destination markets** `/10` |
| **Feasibility** | 30% | 15/30 | Your experience and capacity `/10` · **Experience, quality and contribution of partners/hosts** `/10` · Reasonable budget showing **other revenue sources**, fair payment `/10` |

- **Up to $200,000 per application — but a maximum of 50% of total eligible costs.** Max **$400,000/year**; **6 applications/year**.
- The 50% cap makes "other revenue sources" a *scored* item, not just good practice. Show the other half.
- **Venue/studio/facility rental is only eligible for self-presentation destinations *within Canada*.** Pre-tour production costs only for **remounting existing work**.
- Eligible: artist fees (state whether based on **standard rates set by artists' associations or unions** — i.e. CARFAC-RAAV), project management fees, touring production/exhibition, per diems and accommodation, transport/shipping/crating, **visas and travel insurance**, promotion.
- **Start date must be after the application deadline.**

### 6601 — Micro-grants *(Arts Across Canada and Abroad)*
⚠️ **Internally assessed** by Council staff. **Only two criteria** — the simplest component in the system, and the best entry point for a first-time applicant.

| Criterion | Weight | Min |
|---|---|---|
| **Relevance and impact** | **60%** | 35/60 |
| **Feasibility** | 40% | 25/40 |

- **Up to $10,000 per application**; **3 applications/year**.
- Supports activities advancing an artistic career or practice. Rolling — apply any time before the project or departure.
- No artistic-merit criterion. Argue relevance, impact, and that you can actually do it.

### 6602 — Representation and Promotion *(Arts Across Canada and Abroad)*
⚠️ **Internally assessed — not a peer committee.** Council staff score it. Write it plainer and more factual; there is no room-full-of-artists to charm.

| Criterion | Weight | Min | Sub-criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Relevance** | **40%** | 25/40 | Rationale, including timeliness/suitability `/20` · **Suitability of the event or involvement of partners/hosts** `/20` |
| **Impact** | **40%** | 25/40 | Develop/enhance national or international profile of the artists/practice `/20` · Generate future opportunities `/15` · Build connections and exchange knowledge `/5` |
| **Feasibility** | 20% | 10/20 | Your and your partners' experience and capacity `/10` · Reasonable budget, fair payment `/10` |

- **Up to $30,000**; max **$60,000/year**; **6 applications/year**.
- Eligible: **conference fees and booth rental**, market-development materials, transport, **per diems capped at 5 days per trip**, shipping/baggage, equipment (≤15% of grant).
- **Artistic quality is not a criterion at all here.** This is a market-development grant. Argue the market, not the work.

## Character limits (they are strict, and they are the real brief)

| Field | Limit |
|---|---|
| Describe your proposed activities | **7,000 characters** |
| Project timeline / milestones | 3,500 characters |
| Capacity and experience to carry out activities | 3,500 characters |
| Describe presenters / partners / host and their capacity | 1,700 characters |
| Experience showing you can undertake this project | 1,700 characters |
| Each RECAP question | 1,700 characters |
| Profile/opportunity/connections questions (varies) | 650–1,500 characters |
| Anything else we should know | 1,700 characters |

7,000 characters is roughly 1,100 words — that is the whole project description. Plan the word budget against the criteria weights before writing.

## Support material — the 10-minute rule

> "Assessors are instructed to view as much support material as they need to make an informed decision. **Generally, this means 10 minutes maximum per application.**"

This is the most actionable sentence the Council publishes. Ten minutes covers the *entire* support package. Consequences:

- **Front-load ruthlessly.** The first image and the first 30 seconds of video decide the Artistic score.
- **Do not pad.** Extra material does not buy extra minutes; it spends the same ten on weaker work.
- **Curate toward the proposal**, not toward a career retrospective.
- **CV: 3 pages maximum.** Writing samples: 10–20 pages max. Visual/audio documentation per the form.
- **Test that your files actually open before submitting.**
- **Missing required support material makes the application ineligible** — including the written confirmations from collaborators and partners, which are uploaded *in the support material section*, not the form body.

**Video/file specs (from the portal):** 500 MB maximum · 1080p maximum resolution (frame size 1920 × 1080) · 60 FPS maximum. Exceeding these risks a file that won't play for the assessor — which, inside a 10-minute budget, is the same as not submitting it.

## Equipment purchases — CCA *does* fund them (a real exception)

Contrary to the general "funders don't buy you assets" rule:
- **Artistic Creation:** up to **$2,000 per item**, total combined **≤15% of the requested grant**.
- **Representation and Promotion:** Council-covered portion **≤15% of requested grant**.
- Excludes equipment related to a building.

Enter on the *Equipment Purchase Portion Covered by Canada Council* line.

## Expenses CCA explicitly funds that artists routinely forget to claim

- **Honoraria for Elders and Knowledge Keepers** — including gifts, as thanks for time and knowledge
- **Safety-related costs** — insurance riders, safety gear, on-site specialists
- **Child or dependent care** required to complete the activity (not ongoing/regular care)
- **Public accessibility costs** — sign-language interpretation, captioning, audio description, relaxed performances, access coordinators, ramp rental
- **Travel visas and personnel insurance**

If a project genuinely incurs these, claim them. Omitting them does not make a budget look lean; it makes it look inexperienced.

## If you already hold core funding
Do **not** include expenses already covered by your core grant. Salaries for permanent employees are ineligible for core-funded applicants.
